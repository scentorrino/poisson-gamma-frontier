# Methods Referee Report
**Date:** 2026-05-29
**Paper:** Count Data Stochastic Frontier Models with Gamma Inefficiency
**Paper type:** Theory + Empirics (closed-form distributional theory + MLE + Monte Carlo + one empirical application)
**Design/Approach:** Derivation of a marginal PMF / conditional efficiency score for a Poisson–Gamma frontier (propositions + corollaries), validated by Monte Carlo, applied to one count dataset
**Calibration:** No domain profile, journal profile, or target journal was supplied. Reviewed as a generic top-field-journal methods referee.
**Recommendation:** Major Revisions
**Overall Score:** 68/100

## Summary
The paper derives a closed-form (or one-dimensional-integral) marginal likelihood and conditional technical-efficiency score for a Poisson stochastic frontier with Gamma inefficiency, in both production and cost orientations, with the exponential α=1 case reducing to incomplete gamma functions; it adds a scaling model for inefficiency determinants, characterizes moment-existence regions, validates the MLE by Monte Carlo, and applies it to EPA NPDES non-compliance counts. The theoretical contribution is genuine, clean, and the derivations I checked are correct; the Monte Carlo design is thoughtful (orientation symmetry, moment-existence map, oracle scores). However, the manuscript is **not internally consistent**: the abstract, introduction, and conclusion describe a *patent / Hausman (1984) production-frontier* application that does not exist anywhere in the paper, while §6 is an entirely different *EPA cost-frontier* application — and several headline empirical claims (preferred-model selection, key coefficient signs/significance, mean efficiency) do not survive a look at the actual fitted objects. These are R&R-level fixable but currently dominate the assessment.

## Sanity Check Results
- **Consistency across specs/subsamples:** FAIL at the manuscript level. The described application (patents, production, "capital intensity and science-sector membership are statistically significant determinants," "reproduces the textbook R&D elasticity") appears in the abstract, §1 (line 30), and §7 (lines 26–31), but the *actual* §6 and `code/empirical/cwa_application.R` fit an EPA NPDES **cost** frontier with four completely different determinants (inspection gap, formal enforcement, penalties, percent minority). One of the two narratives is stale. This is the single most serious issue.
- **Coherence (theory+empirics):** Partial. The theory→MC chain coheres well. The theory→empirics chain is broken by the above and by boundary/identification problems below.
- **Sign:** Mostly sensible. The Poisson-GLM intercept-bias sign predictions are confirmed in the MC (verified: predicted ±α·log(b/(b±1)) matches). Empirically, the enforcement-stock δ̂₂>0 "selection" story is coherent.
- **Magnitude:** Concerning. The preferred-model (M5) mean compliance-efficiency score is **0.014** (verified from `results/cwa_fits.rds`), i.e., the average facility is claimed to operate at ~1% of its feasible compliance minimum. §6 line 399 reports "about [X]%" inline, but a mean TE of 1.4% is economically implausible as a "compliance efficiency" and suggests the cost-orientation score is being driven by the boundary α and large b̂, not by a credible inefficiency decomposition. This needs a face-validity defense or a different score normalization.
- **Boundary / fragility:** FAIL. M3 (homogeneous Gamma) returns log α = 2.000, exactly the `upper3` bound in `fit_gamma_manual` (`code/empirical/cwa_application.R:364`). M5 returns log α = 2.664 against an upper bound of 6. The α estimates are therefore at or near optimizer bounds, so the LR test "M3 vs M2 rejects α=1" and the claim that "the data prefer an interior-mode inefficiency density" rest on a constrained estimate, not a free interior optimum.

## Major Comments

1. **The advertised empirical application (patents) is not the one in the paper (EPA NPDES).** The abstract, §1 (line 30: "we apply the model to the @hausman1984 patent panel … capital intensity and science-sector membership are statistically significant determinants of inefficiency … reproduces the textbook R&D elasticity"), and §7 (lines 26–31, which even claim "the homogeneous Gamma fit cannot separately identify α and b" — a finding from a different dataset) are inconsistent with §6, which is an EPA cost-frontier compliance application with n=10,969 facilities and determinants {inspection gap, formal actions, penalties, percent minority}. A referee cannot tell which application the authors intend.
   - **What would change my mind:** Rewrite the abstract, §1 contribution paragraph, the §1 roadmap, and §7 so they describe the EPA cost-frontier application actually estimated in §6 (or, if patents are intended, supply that application). All efficiency/RMSE/coverage claims and the "@hausman1984 panel" references must be reconciled with the code and the saved `cwa_fits.rds`.

2. **The α estimates underpinning the "interior-mode" conclusion are on/near the optimizer boundary.** M3 log α = 2 is exactly the upper bound; M5 log α ≈ 2.66. The central empirical claim of the paper — that the Gamma shape is "empirically relevant" and "the data prefer an interior-mode inefficiency density" (§6 line 367) — is not credible when α̂ is pinned at a constraint. α=exp(2)=7.4 and exp(2.66)=14.4 are extreme shapes that signal the likelihood wants to push α toward a near-degenerate (point-mass) inefficiency, which is observationally close to the negative-binomial / no-frontier limit the paper itself warns about (§4, "as α→0 … reduces to Poisson"; the α→∞ limit is the degenerate-mean case in §3 line 41).
   - **What would change my mind:** Re-fit M3/M5 with the α bound widened (e.g., log α ∈ [−2, 8] consistently across `fit_gamma_manual`, `fit_scaling`, and the package's `[-2,4]`), confirm an interior optimum with a near-zero gradient and a positive-definite Hessian, and report the profile log-likelihood in α. If α̂ genuinely diverges, reinterpret the result (the Gamma is collapsing toward degeneracy) rather than presenting it as evidence for an interior mode.

3. **Inconsistent α bounds across the three estimation paths cast doubt on every Gamma/LR result.** The package `fit.R` uses log α ∈ [−2, 4]; the empirical `fit_gamma_manual` uses [−2, 2]; `fit_scaling` uses [−2, 6]. The §4 prose states log α ∈ [−2, 4] ("α∈[0.14, 55]"). M3's binding at 2 is a direct artifact of the tighter empirical bound. The LR statistics in @tbl-cwa-lr (e.g., M3 vs M2 = 1007, M5 vs M4 = 1196) are computed against likelihoods that may be boundary-constrained.
   - **What would change my mind:** Harmonize the α (and b, δ) bounds across `fit.R`, `fit_gamma_manual`, `fit_scaling`, and the §4 text, re-run, and confirm the LR conclusions are unchanged with interior optima.

4. **Code orientation/documentation is internally contradictory.** `cwa_application.R` is titled and commented throughout as a *production* frontier with an "under-detection / detection-rate" interpretation (lines 4–54, 89, 266, 352, 424), but every fit is called with `orientation = "cost"` (lines 272, 353, 542, 551), and §6 describes a cost frontier with "compliance efficiency." `fit_scaling`'s signature defaults to `orientation = "production"` (line 479) but is always overridden. While the *executed* fits are consistently cost-oriented, the surrounding documentation describes the opposite model, so a replicator reading the script will misunderstand what was estimated.
   - **What would change my mind:** Strip the production/under-detection comments and rename functions/messages to match the cost-frontier specification actually estimated, or document explicitly why a production-labeled script runs cost fits.

5. **Code–paper mismatch on starting values (§4).** §4 ("Starting Values") describes a Poisson-dispersion inversion b₀ = μ̄²/max(Var(Y)−Ȳ, ε) and a fixed cost starter b₀ = e^0.92 ≈ 2.5. The package actually uses a method-of-moments estimator `mom_starts()` that solves an α-free root equation T(b)=T_sample on moment ratios S₂, S₃ (`starts.R`), falling back to b=1/2.5 only on failure. The documented and implemented starting-value strategies are different procedures.
   - **What would change my mind:** Update §4 to describe the `mom_starts` moment-ratio root-finding procedure actually used (it is a reasonable method and arguably better than the prose version), or revert the code to the documented heuristic.

6. **The percent-minority determinant is described as a substantive finding but is economically negligible.** §6 (lines 420, 483) interprets δ̂_minority > 0 as environmental-justice evidence ("high-minority communities host facilities with systematically larger compliance inefficiency"). The estimate is δ̂₄ = +0.0034 (t≈6.3, statistically significant only because n≈11k). A one-SD (≈25 pt) move in percent minority shifts E[u] by ~8.5%, but per the @tbl-cwa-delta-marginals normalization the marginal effect on E[u] is +0.0034·Ē[u]. Statistical significance is being conflated with economic significance.
   - **What would change my mind:** Report a standardized/one-SD marginal effect and a confidence band on the implied efficiency gap across the minority distribution, and temper the EJ interpretation to match the magnitude.

7. **Gamma-frontier delta-method under-coverage (70–80%) is reported but not resolved.** §5 (@fig-sim-coverage, lines 411–416) shows the correctly specified Gamma frontier under-covers β₁ and b at 70–80% and that this "does not attenuate with sample size." This is a real inferential defect of the proposed estimator under correct specification, yet the empirical inference (M3/M5 Wald SEs, the δ significance claims) relies on exactly these delta-method SEs.
   - **What would change my mind:** Diagnose the under-coverage (numerical-Hessian quality vs. genuine non-normality of log α̂; report whether profile-likelihood or bootstrap intervals attain nominal coverage), and use whichever interval is validated for the empirical inference in §6.

## Minor Comments
1. §3 variance-overdispersion justification (lines 41–42) is garbled: "the bracketed term is always positive as $b(b+1)^2 > b^2(b+2)$ implies $b^2 + 2b +1 > b^2 + 2b)$" has an unbalanced parenthesis and the scalar inequality does not by itself establish positivity of the α-dependent bracket $(b/(b+2))^α − (b/(b+1))^{2α}$. The bracket *is* positive (verified numerically for α,b on the grid), but the proof needs the Jensen/convexity argument, not the displayed one-liner.
2. §3 line 21 has a lowercase sentence start ("the MGF of the Gamma…") and "the. the" type fragments; line 41 "($b^2 + 2b)$" leftover.
3. The abstract claims the model "recovers interpretable firm-level technical efficiency scores" for patents — but the only scores computed are the EPA compliance scores with mean 0.014 (see sanity check).
4. §5 line 129 says "$\mp$ sign selecting production / cost" but the DGP @eq-dgp is written with "$\pm$"; the sign-convention prose flips between $\mp$ and $\pm$ across §1, §2, §5 — please make one convention and state it once.
5. @tbl-cwa-main reports M3 α̂ with a delta-method SE; if α̂ is at the boundary the SE and its delta-method transform are not interpretable. Flag boundary fits in the table.
6. `results/cwa_scores_M4.rds`/`M5.rds` are untracked new files (git status); ensure the cached objects shipped match the reported numbers, since the manuscript reads them directly.
7. §6 line 178 and the code: M2/M3 use a fixed cost-starter log(2.5), but `fit_exp_manual` start is `c(beta_pois, log(2.5))` while the package uses `mom_starts`; reconcile which path produced the reported M2/M3.

## Technical Suggestions
1. Add a profile-likelihood plot of the empirical log-likelihood in α (for M3 and M5) to demonstrate an interior maximum, or to document divergence honestly. This is the cleanest way to settle Major Comment 2.
2. For the empirical inference relied on in §6, replace or supplement delta-method SEs with parametric-bootstrap or profile-likelihood intervals for b and α, given the documented MC under-coverage (Major Comment 7).
3. Consider an out-of-estimation moment check for §6: the fitted model implies a specific variance/mean ratio (5.46 in data); report the model-implied overdispersion under M2–M5 as an in-sample-fit / specification diagnostic (the @eq-moments-cost formulas exist but require b>2 — note M2's b̂=0.70 < 1, so the model-implied marginal mean does not exist, which is itself worth stating since it's exactly the moment-existence regime §3 highlights).
4. Add a δ=0 null cell to `montecarlo.R` so the LR test of $H_0:\delta=0$ has a documented size, not only power (the paper repeatedly notes this gap: §5 lines 641–642, 865).
5. Harmonize the bound vectors across `fit.R`, `fit_gamma_manual`, `fit_scaling` into a single shared constant to prevent the kind of boundary artifact found in M3.

## Questions for the Authors
1. Which empirical application is the intended one — the patent/Hausman production frontier described in the abstract/§1/§7, or the EPA NPDES cost frontier in §6? If both, where is the patent application?
2. M3 returns log α̂ = 2.000, exactly the optimizer upper bound. Is the Gamma shape genuinely identified in this dataset, or is the likelihood pushing α toward degeneracy? What does the α-profile look like?
3. A mean compliance-efficiency score of 0.014 under the preferred M5 implies the average facility achieves ~1% of its feasible minimum. Is that economically interpretable, and how is the inline percentage in §6 (line 399) computed relative to this 0.014 mean?
4. M2's b̂ = 0.70 < 1, so per @eq-moments-cost the model-implied marginal mean of Y does not exist for the exponential cost frontier. How should the M2 estimates be interpreted given the outcome's empirical mean is finite (3.18)?
5. Why do the three estimation code paths use different α and b bounds, and were the reported LR tests recomputed after harmonizing them?
6. The §4 starting-value description (dispersion inversion / fixed 2.5) does not match the `mom_starts` moment-ratio procedure in the code. Which was used for the reported results?
